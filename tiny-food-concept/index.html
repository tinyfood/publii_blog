Tiny Food Concept

by Greg Willson
published on July 10, 2019

TinyFood.org strives to educate while improving access to high quality food. Our approach is to support the development of food hubs. Our goals are basic... to make it possible for low income families to afford predominantly organic, locally sourced sustainable foods.

Responsibilities of a food hub

Food hubs are typically coordinated by one person who is compensated in some way for her efforts. The benchmark set by Open Food Network is that food hub managers are paid 15% of the cash or product moving through their hub. This is up to the membersw of each hub, as Tiny Food does not decide decide how your hub is to be run, we simply provide a free online presence tool, with which you may do as you wish. Hub managers typically maintain contact with different food sources and providers, help mentor or provide new farmers with access to good mentors, and reaches out to the general community to ensure that people know far in advance that a group has formed to provide access to local and healthy food. 

Farmers - Farmers can use the "Deploy to Heroku" button to create a new private Tiny Food instance. This service sets up a secure ecommerce system which is ready to integrate with a free Stripe account.  free of charge, and uses a git service like, Github, gitlab, or Bitbucket to host the code which builds the app on heroku.
