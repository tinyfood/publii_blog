North Country Shoppers Hub and North Country Farmer Hub

By Greg Willson
Published on June 16, 2019

Tiny Food got its start talking to farmers in the Canton region, and studying what the Open Food Network had accomplished in its first ten years of steady growth internationally.  There was an expressed need for better visibility for local food sources and access to new markets to help them grow their businesses.  The region is beautiul and has many residents who have grown up with a rich farming history and way of life.

Tiny Food is not a service to assist the elite of our society, rather it is a way to lift up and appreciate those who are the providers. 

We recognize that there is wealth of land knowlege in practice in the north country, and it is our goal to credit those who have worked hard to develop their own methodologies and it is our highest hope that through the use of this project new farmers are connected to good learning opportunities supporting a well informed next generation of sustainable agriculture practitioners.
