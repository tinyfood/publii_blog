FAQ

By Greg Willson
Published on June 19, 2019

We are forming a small food buyers club in our area and would like to know more about how tiny food works.  

At this time, Tiny Food provides some free assistance to groups in setting up new food hubs.  The software we are working with allows several people to be added as staff, and different permissions can be granted to each staff member. Products from different farmers or stores can be associated with different 'collections' which will can easily be linked to from the homepage menus, or images.

What features do you plan to add to the existing setup?

Groups need to be able to more easily manage the content on their homepage. One simple improvement has been to make use of uploaded images to replace the 3 block images and logo that make up the homepage.  We are also working on a config area in the dashboard which will allow for updating of text on the homepage.

Farmers are currently encouraged to create their own producers site, which can be networked as part of a food hub.  

Can I make money running a food hub?

Yes. The model that Open Food Network follows is to provide 15% of the revenue that passes through a food hub as compensation for a food hub manager.  Tiny Food and SCD Hub does not profit from any food hubs, in order to keep accounting simple, yet we encourage you to choose a business model that works for you and that allows you to serve your community best. 

I am a user of Open Food Network. Can Tiny Food's ecommerce system import my product listings?

We would like to take a look,  Saleor tool does have some import tools, but we have not tried it our yet with Spree commerce.  Let us know if you'd like to have help.

 

 

Greg Willson

Greg has worked with technology and community networks since the mid 90s He is the contact person for Tiny Food and available to assist leaders of small groups in making use of the proposed food hub system, or listen to ideas on how it could serve your community better.
