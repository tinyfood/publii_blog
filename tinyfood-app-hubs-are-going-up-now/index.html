Tiny Food Community Food hub service

by Greg Willson
published on June 4, 2019

Tiny Food provides a free, ready-to-use ecommerce service for farmers and food hubs. We reccomend that each group make use of their own private web shop by making use of this launch button,

Please contact greg@scdhub.org so we can link to your hub, and provide assistance with your launch.  We provide limited assistance free of charge. Tiny Food willingly accepts donations, should you be in a position to make a tax deducable charitable donation in support of this work.

The two primary functions of tiny food community food hub service is (1) to allow local people to establish direct ordering relationships from their local farmers, and (2) to support groups of local food buyers to

Your site is yours to own and operate, the ecommerce software is free software, secure hosting of your food hub's web application is possible by the free tier of heroku.com cloud service and git (github.com, gitlab.com, or bitbucket.org)

Branding? You can use your own .com / .org / .x securely via heroku.com for $7/mo if you would rather that than https://{yourhubname}.herokuapp.com      We have customized a few things to make it easier to get started, and are preparing a zip file of all the customizable graphics for the app and web service which you can pass along to a graphic designer or work on yourself.  The free program gimp works well for graphic changes, as does inkscape for making vector (SVG) graphics.

Greg Willson

Greg has worked with technology and community networks since the mid 90s He is the contact person for Tiny Food and available to assist leaders of small groups in making use of the proposed food hub system, or listen to ideas on how it could serve your community better.
